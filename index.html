Context & Explanation

Global Trend

  • Classic "Shift Left" phenomenon.
  • Industry standard: Decrease in Level 1, Stagnation/Increase in Complex tickets.
  • Our status: Significant drop (-47%) proves strategy efficiency.

Multifactorial Causes

  • Digital Democratization: Higher user maturity & younger workforce.
  • Knowledge Management (KM): Success of self-service.
  • Proactive Alerting: Higher stability & fewer duplicate tickets.
  • Shadow Support: Use of Teams and Generative AI.
Strategic Matrix

Strengths

  • Tool Maturity (Self-Healing, KM).
  • Increased availability for complex cases.
  • High Satisfaction & SLA rates.

Weaknesses

  • Less visibility on informal field feedback.
  • Risk of losing reflexes on simple repetitive tasks.

Opportunities

  • Shift from Service Desk to Service Experience.
  • Time for deep-dive projects (Security, Process).
  • Premium Support (VIP / Business Critical).

Threats

  • Budget cuts risk (misinterpretation of volume).
  • Rising complexity of remaining tickets ("Comet Head").

New KPIs (The "Value" Shift)

XLA (Experience Level Agreement)

Measures the outcome (e.g., "Can the user work?") rather than the technical output. Focus on downtime impact.

CES (Customer Effort Score)

"How easy was it to get help?" (Scale 1-5). Targets friction in processes and identifies where Self-Service fails.

Complex Resolution Rate

Since simple tickets are gone, track the ability to solve Level 2/3 issues without escalation to external vendors.

Digital Dexterity Index

Measure of user autonomy and tool adoption. Inverse correlation to ticket volume.
